# AI Search Optimization Skill

Optimize content, brand presence, and technical infrastructure to get **cited and recommended by AI models**: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, Copilot, and autonomous AI agents.

This skill covers the 8 disciplines of AI-native visibility. For traditional search engine optimization (technical SEO, on-page, local SEO, link building, Core Web Vitals), use the companion **ai-seo-skill**.

## Step 3: The 8 Disciplines

### Core AI Optimization

**1. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)**
Getting cited in AI-generated answers across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, and Google AI Overviews. The strategic framework for AI citation. Core tactics: citation hooks (statistic, definition, framework, comparison, authority), original data, author authority signals, 40 to 60 word quotable paragraphs. Reddit is the #1 AI citation source at 40.1% of references.

**2. LLMO (Large Language Model Optimization)**
Optimizing for LLM training data ingestion and RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) retrieval. Technical execution layer with ~80% overlap with GEO. Focus: semantic clarity, clean HTML structure, entity-attribute-value relationships, cross-platform brand mention consistency. LLMO handles the machine-readable side; GEO handles the strategic side.

**3. AIVO (AI Visibility Optimization)**
Ensuring brand accuracy across all AI model responses. 33 to 48% of AI responses contain factual errors about brands. Core method: Golden Prompt audits (15 to 20 prompts tested monthly across 4+ AI platforms), hallucination detection, sentiment monitoring, Share of Model tracking.

**4. CSO (Conversational Search Optimization)**
Optimizing for multi-turn dialogue search patterns. Users ask follow-up questions, and AI models pull from content that anticipates conversation flow. Tactics: question-answer structures, follow-up intent mapping, fanout prompt coverage. 44% of ChatGPT citations come from the first third of content.

### AI Commerce and Agents

**5. AAIO (Agentic AI Optimization)**
Preparing for autonomous AI agents that browse, compare, book, and buy on behalf of users. Agentic traffic increased 1,300% from January to August 2025. Requirements: machine-readable pricing, ReserveAction/BuyAction schema, semantic HTML, clean product/service feeds, API endpoints for agent consumption.

**6. AI Shopping Optimization**
Product visibility in AI-powered shopping: Google AI Mode Shopping, ChatGPT Instant Checkout, Perplexity Buy With Pro. Requirements: structured product feeds (Google Merchant Center), complete Product schema markup, UPC/GTIN identifiers, real-time inventory and pricing data.

### AI Source Channels

**7. Reddit Optimization**
Reddit is the #1 source for AI citations at 40.1% of all AI references. 80M weekly searches happen on Reddit itself. Optimization: subreddit authority building, comment karma accumulation, authentic participation patterns, strategic AMA and post placement. Reddit content feeds directly into ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

**8. Multimodal Search Optimization**
AI platforms increasingly process multiple input types simultaneously: photo + text queries, voice + image, video frame analysis. Content with multi-modal elements sees a +317% AI citation boost. Tactics: descriptive alt text, image-text alignment, video transcripts, structured visual content.

## Step 5: Quick-Start Implementation (4 Weeks)

### Week 1: Audit and Baseline
- Run Golden Prompt audit: test 15 to 20 brand queries across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude
- Document current Share of Model (how often your brand appears vs competitors)
- Log all hallucinations and factual errors
- Audit existing content for citation hooks (stat, definition, framework, comparison, authority)

### Week 2: Citation Infrastructure
- Add/fix Organization, Person, Product/Service schema markup
- Create or update author pages with E-E-A-T signals
- Restructure top 10 pages with 40 to 60 word quotable paragraphs
- Add citation hooks to each page (minimum 2 per page)
- Ensure clean HTML: semantic tags, no render-blocking scripts hiding content

### Week 3: Content and Channels
- Publish 2 to 3 pieces of original data content (surveys, benchmarks, case studies)
- Begin authentic Reddit participation in relevant subreddits
- Add multi-modal elements to top content (images with descriptive alt text, video with transcripts)
- Create FAQ/conversational content for top 5 customer questions

### Week 4: Commerce and Measurement
- If e-commerce: verify product feeds, add complete Product schema with UPC/GTIN
- If services: add ReserveAction schema, machine-readable pricing
- Set up monthly Golden Prompt re-audit schedule
- Establish Share of Model tracking dashboard
- Document baseline metrics for 30/60/90 day comparison

## Step 6: Citation Optimization Checklist

Run this checklist against any page or content piece being optimized for AI citation.

### Content Structure
- [ ] Contains at least 2 citation hooks (statistic, definition, framework, comparison, authority)
- [ ] Has 40 to 60 word quotable paragraphs (the ideal length for AI extraction)
- [ ] Key claims appear in the first third of the content (44% of citations come from there)
- [ ] Uses clear entity-attribute-value statements ("X is Y" or "X does Y")
- [ ] Includes original data, proprietary research, or unique frameworks
- [ ] Question-answer pairs for conversational search patterns

### Technical Foundation
- [ ] Clean semantic HTML (proper heading hierarchy, no content hidden by JS)
- [ ] Organization schema with complete properties
- [ ] Author page linked with Person schema and E-E-A-T credentials
- [ ] Page loads without JavaScript rendering required for content access
- [ ] Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) across all web properties

### Multi-Modal
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text (not keyword-stuffed, genuinely descriptive)
- [ ] Video content has full transcripts embedded on page
- [ ] Visual content reinforces text claims (not decorative stock photos)

### Brand Accuracy
- [ ] Brand name spelled consistently across all platforms
- [ ] Key brand facts stated explicitly (founding year, location, offerings)
- [ ] No contradictory information across different pages or platforms
- [ ] Wikipedia/Wikidata entry accurate (if applicable)

### Commerce (if applicable)
- [ ] Product schema with UPC/GTIN, price, availability, reviews
- [ ] Machine-readable pricing (not trapped in images or PDFs)
- [ ] ReserveAction or BuyAction schema for bookable/purchasable items
- [ ] Product feed submitted to Google Merchant Center
